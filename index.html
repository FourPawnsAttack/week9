Exploring The Health of The Great Barrier Reef

What is the Great Barrier Reef?

The Great Barrier Reef (GBR), located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, is the world's largest coral reef system, stretching over 2,300km. It is home to a diverse ecosystem, including thousands of species of marine life, such as fish & corals. The reef plays a critical role in supporting biodiversity and protecting coastlines from erosion. Additionally, it contributes significantly to Australia's economy through tourism and fishing industries. However, it faces serious threats from climate change, pollution, and coral bleaching, which endanger its long-term survival. Its protection is crucial for maintaining ecological balance and preserving marine habitats.
